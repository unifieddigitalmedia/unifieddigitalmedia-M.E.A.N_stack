The digital world in which Unified Digital Media operates, has undergone great changes, mainly in the consumption of Information Technology. The IT environment for brands has changed enormously over the past ten years.

 

Taken together, these developments open up different and potentially more effective ways of working.

 

Three prevalent trends are bringing about this transformation:

  • The growing popularity of consumer technology among business users
  • The shift of business application and data to cloud.
  • Confluence of social, mobile, analytic and cloud computing creating new ways to develop products and interact with customers.
 

The enduring mission of Unified Digital Media is to create positives attitudes to brands through the provision of good online experiences.

Each project is distinctive, the management process :

  • Knows who is important and delivers relevant personalised experiences
  • Pays attention to your customers and piques their interest from the start
  • Makes it personal and keeps visitors interested and coming back
  • Becomes accessible across devices and social networks
  • Builds trust by employing permission based marketing when possible
  • Interprets quantitative analytics and qualitative visitor's feedback.
  • Increases online activity by using recommendations
  • Continuously improves the online experience to convert visitors into loyal customers

ABOUT US

Unified Digital Media London is a IT service provider founded by Machel Slack MSc Bsc (Hons) in 2012. Graduating from Kingston University (UK) in 2008, Machel Slack visited Jamaica, where he successfully implemented an IT system within the University of the West Indies' Department of Educational Studies.

There he was responsible for a group of staff sharing digital technology knowledge with faculty and IT managers to ensure streamlining of students’ cashless transactions, as part of the University’s strategic objective. Having completed his stint within that Department, he was asked to act as a liaison person, carrying out analytical work designing and developing a new system with software vendors. That work resulted in the implementation of an IT system within one of the University's commercial entities.

Later he returned to the UK, where he continued his education with Kingston University (UK). Having completed his Masters programme, he went on to establish Unified Digital Media.
